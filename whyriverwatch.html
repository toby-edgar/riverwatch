Why RiverWatch

RiverWatch is the only water quality monitoring system that provides intelligent data interpretation.

Our Mission:


Declining water quality is a reality we have all been witnessing for some decades. Rivers and streams that once were an abundant resource, have had a dramatic degradation in health and productivity. Lakes that we enjoyed swimming in are now green with algae or brown with sediment. This was the case for the Pahaoa River that runs around RiverWatch co-founder Grant Muir's farm in the Wairarapa. In 2002, it was alive and thriving. Ten years later in 2012, the river was dead and devoid of aquatic life.


The story of the struggle to save the river compelled James Muir to make the documentary film River Dog, which received critical acclaim around the globe. The success of the documentary, and the attention it gained for the Pahaoa River, lead James and Grant to explore more ways in which they could help reverse the decline of freshwater quality in Aotearoa. In 2012 they set up the not-for-profit company WAI NZ - Water Action Initiative New Zealand.


They soon discovered how little information was being collected on the state of our waterways. Only 8% of our rivers and only 5% of our lakes are monitored. The traditional methods for measuring water quality are time consuming and expensive. Additionally, the limited data that is available isn’t robust, because it is typically collected at a single point in space and time. But as a river is a system in constant flux, the best way to observe and monitor changes in water quality would be to measure it continuously. So, Grant and James set out to invent a device that would gather and communicate water quality data in real-time.


The first RiverWatch prototype was awarded the WWF Conservation Innovation Award in 2016, & again in 2017, with WAI NZ becoming a finalist in the Wellington Gold Awards that same year. In 2018 RiverWatch secured R&D funding and support from Foundation North and Callaghan Innovation, allowing Grant and James to take their invention to the next level, with the hydrodynamic waka design. RiverWatch has been testing the new technology and has recently won more awards for its innovation (AWS Innovation in data award 2019).

Our Impact:


RiverWatch has at its core, a mission to help understand and manage water quality. Founded in response to the growing water quality crisis, the company serves the public good by providing services and data, aiding rapid responses to stop activities which damage waterways.


The aim is that we collect data and create a benchmark for water quality monitoring through the use of intelligent software. We can achieve our impact objectives of social change through education and support of environmental monitoring. Thus assisting in the action and delivery of meaningful and measurable change in water quality management.


Our vision for RiverWatch is to have partnerships with leading organisations, companies, government and individuals who share the commitment too monitoring, protecting, restoring and sharing information on our waterways. That RiverWatch is the data centre for these efforts and our collection and dissemination of data is integral to peoples connection with and understanding of water quality.


RiverWatch aims to become recognised as a company with high standards for Zero Waste, Product Stewardship, Data transparency, Impact Business models and new ways of structuring the business hierarchy. This is an opportunity to reinvent how we operate collectively and in cooperation. Create Impact for the good of the planet, create jobs, create space for invention and ideas, create healthy environments, create impact.

There's a RiverWatch for everyone.


About

RiverWatch is a Social Enterprise committed to the restoration and enhancement of Freshwater. We are engaged in providing communities and businesses with the resources and support needed to monitor entire catchments. Our aim is to become Zero Waste, Carbon Positive and to have a full circle product stewardship.
